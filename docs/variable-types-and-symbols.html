Since \(X_j\) is \(n\times p\) column vector, although we called \(X\) as a matrix of \(n\times p\), actually, \(X\) is \(X^T\). \[ \begin{align} input \ vector: X_j & = \begin{bmatrix} x_{j,1}\\x_{j,2}\\ ..\\ x_{j,p} \\ \end{bmatrix}\\ input \ matrix: X_{n\times p} &= \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,1},x_{1,2},......x_{1,p} \\ x_{2,1},x_{2,2},......x_{2,p} \\ .................. \\ x_{n,1},x_{n,2},......x_{n,p} \end{bmatrix}\\ &=\begin{bmatrix} X_1^T\\ X_2^T\\ .\\ .\\ X_n^T \end{bmatrix}\\ estimation &=\hat Y \end{align} \]
